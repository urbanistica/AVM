Environmental Design That Accelerates Learning.

Most classrooms focus on line-of-sight from teacher to students and back. In AVM classrooms, we focus on student-to-student visibility.

What seems like a small thing; desks in AVM classrooms are arranged in an arc of 3 together. But this is a key part of our challenge-driven curriculum.

Providing a line of sight for each student every other, within each team of six students, the social learning aspects of AVM are reinforced by these simple arrangents.

Each desk has one workstation; two students share the desk, and the workstation. You can read more about how this promotes social learning.

Suprisingly, students generally have their back to the teacher, who are positioned where they can still oversee the classroom

But because the focus is on students acquiring skills and knowledge by working together, and not by focusing on a kind of authoritative teacher figure, the need for traditional ideas about teacher placement is reduced.

The underlying message to students is simple: the ability to discover, question and learn lies within you, and your fellow learners.

Observably in our completed courses, this develops in students a sense of respect, reliance, and a sense of genuine accomplishment and pride in themselves and in their fellow learners.

On the teaching side, the teaching load is reduced, and the teachers require less training and preparation to effectively guide students through the course.
